Overview

Business Infinity runs a persistent AI boardroom across your ERP, MES, CRM, and SaaS stack — where domain‑tuned C‑suite agents deliberate with governance, execute guarded actions, and learn from outcomes. It collapses the gap between agreement and execution, and turns every decision into compounding capability.

Decision power

  • Always‑on: Continuous deliberation beats meeting cadence.
  • Quorum: Agents propose, contest, and converge.
  • Explainable: Rationale and trade‑offs are first‑class.

Execution

  • Guarded writes: Dry‑runs, approvals, rollback.
  • Blast radius: Canary, caps, scopes, kill switch.
  • Traceable: Decision‑to‑action lineage.

Learning

  • Decision ledger: Outcomes feed playbooks.
  • Evaluator commons: Reusable tests of “what good looks like.”
  • Network effect: Patterns propagate by choice.

Domain‑tuned agents

Specialized agents operate with explicit charters, KPIs, and risk budgets. They deliberate as a quorum, with humans able to set constraints, request justifications, and gate actions.

Agent charter template (YAML) 
boardroom:
  name: "Revenue & Resilience Council"
  slas:
    decision_latency: "≤ 4h medium-impact; ≤ 24h high-impact"
agents:
  - role: "CRO Agent"
    kpis: ["ARR", "NRR", "Win rate"]
    powers: ["Pricing experiments", "Pipeline prioritization"]
    limits:
      discount_floor: "≥ 18%"
      experiment_exposure: "≤ 5%"
  - role: "COO Agent"
    kpis: ["On-time", "Cycle time", "Cost-to-serve"]
    powers: ["Schedule rebalancing", "Supplier risk mitigation"]
    limits:
      overtime_cap: "≤ 10%"
Deliberation and quorum
  • Proposal/Challenge: Agents generate options and critique assumptions.
  • Scoring: Options scored against KPIs and risk constraints.
  • Quorum: Decision passes when rules of agreement are met.

Decision ledger

Every decision is a first‑class object: machine‑readable and human‑auditable.

Schema

Orchestration & actions

Guarded action adapters write changes into your systems with policy enforcement and safety rails.

Safety

  • Dry‑run: Diff previews before apply.
  • SoD: Proposer ≠ approver ≠ executor for high risk.
  • Rollback: Automatic reversion plans.

Policy gates

  • Finance: Budget, margin, revenue recognition checks.
  • Privacy: PII/PHI handling and masking.
  • Compliance: Regional and industry rules.

Controls

  • Caps: Exposure, spend, and scope limits.
  • Canary: Progressive rollout and monitoring.
  • Kill: Hard stop on violation or drift.

FAQ

How is this different from dashboards or copilots?

Dashboards inform; copilots assist. Business Infinity deliberates, decides, and acts — under governance — and learns from outcomes to raise future decision quality.

What’s the first safe step?

Read‑only connectors, replayable simulations, and a decision ledger you can put in front of audit. Then enable guarded writes in a limited scope with rollback.

How do you protect privacy?

Least‑privilege access, redaction for inter‑boardroom contexts, regional residency, and customer‑managed keys where required.
